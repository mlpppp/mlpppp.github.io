COMP 5704: Parallel Algorithms and Applications in Data Science

 

School of Computer Science
Carleton University, Ottawa, Canada

Project Title: Parallelizing the training of sequential and linear models

Name: Wei Li

E-Mail: Wli206@uottawa.ca

Project Outline: Given the ubiquity of large-scale data solutions and the availability of low-commodity clusters, distributing learning models that are linear in nature or using linear based optimization methods like SGD to speed it up is an obvious choice. However, many parallelized solutions to those problems either result in inferior model quality(ie. divergence) or low training efficiency. In recent researches a common statement that the convergence rate is a function of the sparsity and suboptimal communication between workers can lead to poor convergence is established. This project aims at understanding the nature of the phenomenon and following the researches that try to resolve this problem and reproduce some of the state-of-the-art.
